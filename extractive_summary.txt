Learning software design is important for innovation, technology advancement, problem-solving, career opportunities, automation, creativity, adaptability, critical thinking, logical reasoning, and entrepreneurial opportunities. Designing software involves creating artifacts such as roadmaps, databases, data models, printed documents, and scripts that help describe the architecture and design functions. The personal computing era of the 1970s and 1980s marked the arrival of personal computers, such as Apple’s revolutionary Apple II in April 1977. Problems in software development include unclear requirements, inadequate communication, confidentiality issues, bugs in final products, and hidden costs. A wicked problem is characterized by its form and solution being interconnected so that solving one aspect affects the problem itself. Software is a set of instructions, data, or programs used to operate computers and perform specific tasks. Open-source software entered the mainstream in the 1990s, with the Linux kernel forming the basis of the Linux operating system released in 1991. Software development is a cooperative process between humans and computers that involves requirements gathering, designing solutions, implementation, testing, deployment, maintenance, and support. The design phase involves envisioning and defining solutions, designing modules, specifying control relationships, defining interfaces, structuring data, and creating necessary algorithms.
