In the early days of software, Tom Kilburn, a computer scientist, was responsible for writing the world’s first piece of software, which ran on June 21, 1948, at the University of Manchester, England. Fortran was one of the first higher-level programming languages and was originally published in 1957. VisiCalc, the first spreadsheet software for professional computing, became popularly known as the Apple II’s killer app and first appeared in 1979. Open-source software emerged as a major innovation in the history of software development and entered the mainstream in the 1990s. Making and using tools is a fundamental human characteristic that incorporates elements of design activity. Software development artifacts help describe the architecture and design functions of software. Translating a design into a product almost always requires communication between designers and development teams. Problem-solving in design involves defining the problem, determining its cause, identifying solutions, selecting an alternative, and implementing the solution. Abstraction plays a key role in software design by simplifying details and focusing on essential elements. Software is a set of instructions, data, or programs used to operate computers and execute specific tasks. Objectives of software design include correctness, efficiency, understandability, completeness, and maintainability. Software development is a cooperative activity carried out between humans and computers to create solutions through requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, deployment, and maintenance.
